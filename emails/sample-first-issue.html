Recipe of the Month

One-Pan Spring Vegetable Risotto

Seasonal, low-effort, and perfect for using up those pantry staples. This recipe works brilliantly with GrubShelf's meal planning — add it to your week and ingredients drop straight onto your list.

🍚

Spring Vegetable Risotto

Serves 4 · 35 minutes · One pan

Ingredients

•  300g arborio rice
•  1L warm vegetable stock
•  1 leek, finely sliced
•  150g asparagus, trimmed & chopped
•  100g frozen peas
•  60g parmesan, grated
•  2 tbsp butter
•  2 cloves garlic, minced
•  Zest of 1 lemon
•  Salt, pepper & olive oil

Method

1. Heat olive oil and 1 tbsp butter in a wide pan over medium heat. Sauté leek and garlic until soft, about 3 minutes.

2. Add rice and stir for 1 minute until edges turn translucent. Pour in stock all at once (yes, really — no ladling needed).

3. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook for 18 minutes, stirring occasionally.

4. Stir in asparagus and peas. Cook uncovered for 4–5 minutes until tender.

5. Remove from heat. Fold in parmesan, remaining butter, and lemon zest. Season to taste and serve immediately.
Pantry Tips

3 Habits That Cut Food Waste in Half

The average household throws away $1,500 of food each year. Here are three small changes GrubShelf users swear by:

1️⃣ First In, First Out

When you unpack groceries, move older items to the front of the fridge and shelf. GrubShelf's pantry view already sorts by date added — use it as your real-world guide too.

2️⃣ Plan "Leftover Nights"

Designate one evening a week as a use-it-up night. Check your GrubShelf pantry for items expiring soon and build a meal around them. You'll be surprised how creative you get.

3️⃣ Freeze Before It's Too Late

Bread, bananas, herbs, cooked grains — all freeze beautifully. If GrubShelf shows something in your pantry that you won't use this week, pop it in the freezer and update the item.
